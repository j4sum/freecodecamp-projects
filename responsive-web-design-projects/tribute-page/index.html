Ruth Pfau

The woman who served the unserved

  • 1929 - Born in Leipzig Germany
  • 1948 - Escaped Soviet Occupation of East Germany and moved to West Germany
  • 1960 - The order sent her to India but due to visa issues she landed in Karachi. She chose to stay in Pakistan after witnessing the destruction caused by leprosy. Having found no treatment or hospitals for patients suffering from leprosy, Dr Ruth collected donations form Pakistan and Germany.
  • 1962 - Social work department of Marie Adelaide Leprosy Center (MALC) run by Dr Ruth, was founded by Dr. I. K. Gill
  • 1963 - Leprosy clinic was bought with patients from Karachi, from elsewhere in Pakistan, and even from Afghanistan coming in for treatment. In two years MALC was transferred to a proper hospital building and established a full-service leprosy treatment and rehabilitation center, free to patients.
  • 1968 - Convinced the Pakistani government to start a National Leprosy Control Program in corporation with the Marie Adelaide Leprosy Center (MALC) and set up clinics all over the country.
  • 1969 - received Order of Merit, Nishan-e-Quaid-i-Azam
  • 1979 - Awarded Hilal-e-Imtiaz
  • 1988 - Awarded Pakistani citizenship
  • 1989 - Received Hilal-i-Pakistan and visited Afghanistan to work with leprosy patients
  • 1996 - WHO declares Pakistan leprosy free
  • 2002 - Ramon Magsaysay Award
  • 2003 - Jinnah Award
  • 2004 - Awarded Doctor of Science Honoris Causa Aga Khan University, Karachi
  • 2005 - Marion Doenhoff Prize
  • 2015 - German Staufer Medal
  • 2017 - Died at age 87
"It is better to light a candle than curse the dark" --Ruth Pfau
